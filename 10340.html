Korpi Bemoans Low Attendance in Sochi

SOCHI, December 7 (R-Sport) - Finnish figure skater Kiira Korpi said Friday she was disappointed by the low attendance at the Grand Prix Finals in Sochi, held at the future 2014 Olympic arena.

Korpi skated to fourth in the short program Friday in a 12,000-capacity Iceberg Skating Palace that was less than half full despite promises of a sellout.

"Actually i was expecting a bit more audience because someone told me it would be sold out," said Korpi, the first Finn to skate at the finals. 

"Hopefully for the Olympics it will be more crowded."

International Skating Union president Ottavio Cinquanta raised the issue Thursday and expressed concerns that those who had bought tickets may not come to the event.

The finals are the first international test event at the Olympic Park.
